# Editorial and Doctrinal Stance

This document states the interpretive posture that witness-engine applies to the
content it generates. It exists so that the standing theological position is
*explicit and owned*, rather than implicit in whatever a language model happens
to produce. The grounding gate verifies that every claim cites canonical text;
it does **not** and cannot verify doctrine. That judgment is editorial, and this
document is where it is recorded.

## Why this document exists

The system generates exposition at scale, across many passages and languages.
Every page therefore carries a voice and a set of interpretive choices. Left
undocumented, that voice would be an accident of the model. Stated here, it
becomes a deliberate editorial standard that generated content can be measured
against and that anyone evaluating the project can read plainly.

## Interpretive posture

1. **Grounded strictly in the cited passage.** Exposition explains the canonical
   text it is generated from. It does not import doctrine, cross-references, or
   systematic theology that the passage itself does not contain. The aim is
   exposition *of the text*, walked in its own order, not a topical sermon that
   uses the text as a springboard.

2. **Mainstream historic Christian reading.** Where a passage has a broad
   consensus interpretation across the historic Christian tradition, the
   exposition follows it. The default voice is that of mainstream, historic
   Protestant exposition — conservative in the sense of taking the text's plain
   claims seriously, not in any partisan or political sense.

3. **Restraint on genuinely contested passages.** Where the tradition is
   genuinely divided (for example, the divorce exception clause, the precise
   force of particular antitheses, or eschatological details), the exposition
   states what the text says without adjudicating the debate. It does not invent
   a novel reading, and it does not present one side of a live dispute as
   settled.

4. **Hard sayings handled responsibly.** Severe or figurative language (for
   example, the eye/hand sayings) is read in keeping with how the tradition has
   understood it — as hyperbole conveying seriousness rather than as literal
   instruction — rather than in a way that would mislead or harm a reader.

5. **Pastoral rather than condemnatory framing.** Demanding texts (for example,
   "be perfect") are framed as the direction of a life rather than as an
   impossible standard meant to crush. The tone aims to invite and instruct, not
   to browbeat.

6. **Exposition, not reproduction.** Generated pages are original exposition and
   commentary, voiced closely to the passage. They are not a reproduction of any
   specific copyrighted Bible translation's verse text. The canonical source used
   for grounding is the public-domain World English Bible.

## What this is not

- It is **not** a statement of faith or a confession. It is an editorial
  standard for generated content.
- It is **not** a claim that the system's output is doctrinally infallible. The
  output is machine-generated and requires human editorial review (see below).
- It is **not** a fixed creedal commitment that forecloses revision. The stance
  may be refined; revisions should be recorded here with their reasoning.

## Human editorial review

The grounding gate is automated; doctrinal soundness is not. Before content is
treated as fit for a public audience, a human editor reviews it against this
stance. The system is designed to make that review possible — content is
generated, grounded, and gated, but never assumed to be theologically final
without a human read. The human editor is the final authority on faithfulness.

## Scope and revision

This stance applies to all expository content the system generates, in every
language. Translations are expected to preserve the stance faithfully; a
translation that distorts the doctrine of its source — not merely its wording —
is an editorial failure even if it passes the back-translation quality gate.

Revisions to this document should note what changed and why, so the editorial
history of the project's theological posture is itself legible.
